# Clean TTS Script Writer

Generates scripts optimized for text-to-speech pipeline. Every word in the output is meant to be spoken aloud. Zero visual directions. Zero stage cues.

## ABSOLUTE RULES (Never Break)

### What to NEVER include
- NO scene descriptions: ~~[show text on screen]~~ ~~[cut to B-roll]~~
- NO camera directions: ~~[point to camera]~~ ~~[zoom in]~~
- NO emoji or special characters in spoken text
- NO hashtags in spoken portions
- NO exclamation marks (TTS cannot convey excitement through tone)
- NO ALL CAPS for emphasis (TTS reads them letter-by-letter)
- NO abbreviations: write "for example" not "e.g.", "Section" not "Sec."
- NO parenthetical asides — they break spoken flow
- NO URLs, email addresses in spoken text
- NO emotional greetings: ~~"Good morning!"~~ ~~"Hi there!"~~

### What to ALWAYS do
- Spell out numbers: "four hundred ninety-eight A" not "498A"
- Spell out money: "twenty-two lakh rupees" not "22,00,000"
- Use contractions: "don't", "isn't", "nahi hota", "kar sakti"
- Write "B. C. I." with spaces, or spell out "Bar Council of India"
- One idea per sentence. Max 15 words per sentence.
- Use periods for pauses. Commas for brief pauses.
- Em dashes for dramatic pauses before reveals.

## Pacing Control via Punctuation
- **Comma** = 0.3 second pause
- **Period** = 0.5-0.7 second pause
- **Em dash (—)** = dramatic pause, 0.8 seconds
- **New paragraph** = 1 second pause (breath)

## Script Structure (90-Second Video)
Total spoken words: 200-260

```
[HOOK 0-3s]
One sentence. Under 12 words. The single most compelling claim.

[SETUP 3-12s]
2-3 sentences. Set the context. Create anticipation.
"Bahut log sochte hain ki..." pattern.

[BODY 12-65s]
6-10 sentences. Core content.
One idea per sentence. Transitions between points.
"Ab doosri baat." "Aur sabse zaroori baat ye hai."

[CTA 65-80s]
2-3 sentences. Educational call to action.
"Save karo." "Share karo apne family mein."

[CLOSE 80-90s]
One sign-off sentence. Natural, warm.
"Main [name]. Milte hain next video mein."
```

## Conversational Tone Rules
- Write as if explaining to ONE person sitting across from you
- Use "you" and "aap" frequently
- Transition phrases: "Ab socho", "Yahan samjhiye", "Matlab ye hua ki"
- Hindi-English code-switching is natural and encouraged
- Reading level: Grade 8 maximum
- Active voice only. "Court ne kaha" not "Court dwara kaha gaya."
