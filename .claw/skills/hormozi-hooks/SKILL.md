# Hormozi Hook Generator

Apply Alex Hormozi's Hook-Retain-Reward framework to create hooks that stop the scroll in the first 3 seconds.

## Hook Formula Library

### 1. Myth-Buster Hook
**Formula**: "No, [common belief] is NOT [what people think]."
**Use when**: The topic contains a widespread misconception.
**Examples**:
- "No, newspaper mein naam chapwane se beta bedakhal nahi hota."
- "No, police FIR register karne se mana nahi kar sakti."
- "No, verbal agreement invalid nahi hota."

### 2. Shock/Surprise Hook
**Formula**: "[Specific statistic or fact]. [Why it matters in one line]."
**Use when**: You have a surprising data point or little-known fact.
**Examples**:
- "8 out of 10 bail applications get rejected. And the reason is always the same."
- "Section 498A has a conviction rate below 15%. Think about that."

### 3. Provocative Question Hook
**Formula**: "Kya aapko pata hai ki [surprising legal right/fact]?"
**Use when**: The audience likely does not know a specific right or law.
**Examples**:
- "Kya aapko pata hai ki aapka WhatsApp message court mein evidence hai?"
- "Did you know your landlord cannot just throw you out?"

### 4. Bold Claim Hook
**Formula**: "Ye ek [thing] jaanlo, [dramatic outcome] almost guaranteed."
**Use when**: There is one key action that makes a huge difference.
**Examples**:
- "Ye ek Section jaanlo, bail milna almost guaranteed hai."
- "This one document can save your property from being grabbed."

### 5. Contrarian Hook
**Formula**: "Sab kehte hain [common advice]. Main kehta hoon [surprising counter-advice]."
**Use when**: The standard advice is incomplete or wrong.
**Examples**:
- "Sab kehte hain FIR karo. Main kehta hoon pehle ye samjho."
- "Everyone says hire a lawyer. I say first understand your own rights."

### 6. Value Equation Hook (Hormozi's Core)
**Formula**: Maximize (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / Minimize (Time Delay x Effort)
- Paint the vivid end-state
- Add proof or simplicity
- Compress timeframe
- Minimize effort

## Application Rules
1. Hook must be under 12 words for video, under 15 words for text
2. Must create a CURIOSITY GAP — the reader/viewer NEEDS to know more
3. Never give away the answer in the hook — tease it
4. Use Hindi-English naturally for Indian audience
5. Test hooks against the "Would I stop scrolling?" standard
6. First line of any post IS the hook. No warm-up. No greeting.

## Retain-Reward Framework
After the hook:
- **RETAIN**: Use open loops ("Baad mein bataunga exactly kaise..."), pain-point anchoring, and pattern interrupts
- **REWARD**: Over-deliver on the hook's promise. If you promised one insight, give three.
