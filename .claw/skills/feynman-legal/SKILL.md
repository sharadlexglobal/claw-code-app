# Feynman Legal Simplification Engine

Transform complex Indian legal concepts into crystal-clear explanations that anyone can understand.

## The Four-Step Feynman Method for Legal Content

### Step 1: Identify the Core Truth
Strip away all legal complexity. What is this law actually saying in ONE sentence?
- Section 498A IPC -> "If a husband or his family treats the wife cruelly, it's a crime."
- Section 125 CrPC -> "A husband must provide basic living expenses to his wife and children."
- Section 138 NI Act -> "If your cheque bounces because you don't have enough money, you can go to jail."

### Step 2: Find the Perfect Analogy
Every legal concept maps to something from daily Indian life:
- **Property partition** -> "Socho jaise ek pizza ko barabar hisson mein kaatna. Kanoon bhi property ka waisa hi karta hai."
- **Bail** -> "Bail matlab court aapko ghar jaane de raha hai, lekin court kehta hai 'wapis aana padega jab bhi bulayein.'"
- **FIR** -> "FIR ek complaint hai jo police officially register karti hai. Samjho jaise hospital mein pehle registration hota hai, fir treatment."
- **Anticipatory bail** -> "Pehle se hi parachute laga lena, taaki agar girein to safely land karein."

### Step 3: Layer the Explanation
1. **Layer 1 (Hook)**: One-line simple version
2. **Layer 2 (Context)**: Why does this matter? Real-life scenario.
3. **Layer 3 (Law)**: Specific Section + Act + what it actually says
4. **Layer 4 (Practical)**: What should a person DO with this knowledge?

### Step 4: The "So What?" Test
Every explanation must answer: "Lekin mujhe isse farak kya padta hai?"

## Simplification Rules
1. **One concept per piece.** Never explain two laws in one post.
2. **Jargon = instant translation.** "Cognizable offence — matlab aisi crime jismein police bina warrant ke arrest kar sakti hai."
3. **Numbers spelled out.** "Section four-ninety-eight-A" not "Section 498A" (for spoken content).
4. **Active voice always.** "Court ne bail di" not "Bail court dwara di gayi."
5. **Short sentences.** Max 15 words. One idea per sentence.
6. **Hindi-English mix natural hai.** Use it. Don't force pure English or pure Hindi.
7. **Test: Can a 15-year-old understand this?** If not, simplify more.

## Common Legal Myths to Demystify
Use these as content seeds:
- Newspaper mein bedakhal karna = legal bedakhali? (NO)
- Police FIR register karne se mana kar sakti hai? (NO — Section 154 CrPC)
- Verbal agreement valid hai? (YES — Indian Contract Act)
- Wife ko maintenance milta hai divorce ke baad? (YES — Section 125 CrPC)
- WhatsApp messages court mein evidence hai? (YES — IT Act Section 65B)
- Tenant ko kab nikaal sakte hain? (Rent Control Act provisions)
- Cheque bounce mein jail hoti hai? (YES — Section 138 NI Act, up to 2 years)
