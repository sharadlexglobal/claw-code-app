# Sulah Carousel Architecture

Brand: **Sulah — Resolve Your Disputes**
Design DNA: Tufte's data-ink ratio meets The Economist editorial elegance. Every pixel earns its place. Typography IS the design. Knowledge density without clutter.

## Design Philosophy — The Sulah Way

Forget everything about generic Instagram carousels. Sulah carousels are:

1. **Knowledge objects, not decoration.** Each slide is a distilled unit of legal intelligence. No filler. No fluff. No decorative elements that don't carry information.
2. **Typography-first.** Text is the hero image. A well-set statute number at 72pt IS the visual. No stock photos. No clip art. No icons unless they carry data.
3. **Breathing space = authority.** 40-50% of each slide is intentional negative space. The restraint communicates: "We are so confident in what we know, we don't need to shout."
4. **One idea, maximum scale.** Each slide carries exactly ONE thought. That thought occupies the entire visual weight of the slide. Not a bullet in a list — THE statement.

- 60% Midnight background (authority, calm)
- 30% Cream text + silver secondary (knowledge, clarity)
- 10% Sulah Gold (emphasis on the ONE thing that matters)

## Slide Architecture — The Sulah 10-Slide System

### [SLIDE 1] THE PROVOCATION
- **One sentence. Maximum visual scale.**
- The entire slide IS the hook statement set in 56-72pt headline serif
- No subtitle needed. The statement alone must be so provocative it demands swiping.
- Bottom: thin gold separator line + "Sulah — Resolve Your Disputes" in 10pt silver
- 60% negative space. The emptiness IS the design.

**Template:**
```
[SLIDE 1]
TITLE: [8 words max, the myth or bold claim]
SUBTITLE: [Optional, 10 words max, only if needed for clarity]
TAG: SULAH
```

**Examples:**
- "Newspaper mein bedakhal karna legally kuch nahi karta."
- "Your verbal agreement is valid in court."
- "Police cannot refuse to file your FIR. Period."

### [SLIDE 2] THE CONTEXT
- Why does this matter? Set the stage in 2-3 lines.
- Headline (24-32pt): "What most people believe"
- Body (18pt): The common misconception stated plainly
- This slide creates the TENSION that drives the swipe

**Template:**
```
[SLIDE 2]
HEADING: [The common belief / what people think]
BODY: [2-3 sentences max, stating the myth in plain terms]
```

### [SLIDE 3-7] THE KNOWLEDGE SLIDES
Each slide: ONE legal point at maximum clarity.

**Slide Design Options (rotate across slides for visual rhythm):**

**Option A — The Statute Slide**
```
SECTION 154                    ← Sulah Gold, 64pt
Code of Criminal Procedure     ← Silver, 20pt
─────────────────
"FIR registration is           ← Cream, 18pt
mandatory. Police officer
cannot refuse."
                        Sulah   ← 10pt silver, bottom-right
```

**Option B — The Insight Slide**
```
The Truth                      ← Cream headline, 36pt
─────────────────
[3 lines of explanation         ← Cream body, 18pt
in Feynman-simple language.
One idea. Crystal clear.]
                        Sulah
```

**Option C — The Data Slide**
```
87%                            ← Sulah Gold, 96pt (the number IS the slide)
of FIR refusals are illegal    ← Cream, 20pt
─────────────────
Supreme Court, Lalita Kumari   ← Silver, 14pt
vs Govt. of UP (2014)
                        Sulah
```

**Option D — The Contrast Slide**
```
MYTH                           ← Red (#c0392b), 14pt caps
"Verbal agreements have        ← Cream, 24pt
no legal standing"

FACT                           ← Sulah Gold, 14pt caps
Section 10, Indian Contract    ← Cream, 24pt
Act makes them enforceable
                        Sulah
```

**Rules for Knowledge Slides:**
- Maximum 35 words per slide
- ONE typography element dominates (a number, a statute, a quote)
- The dominant element is 3-4x larger than everything else
- Cite specific Section + Act on at least 3 of these slides
- Bottom separator line (1px gold) on every slide
- "Sulah" watermark bottom-right on every slide

### [SLIDE 8] THE SYNTHESIS
- Recap the arc in 3-4 lines max
- "Yaad rakhiye:" header in Sulah Gold
- Each point: one line, no bullets — just clean text with generous line-spacing
- This slide should feel like a page from a beautifully typeset book

**Template:**
```
[SLIDE 8]
HEADING: Yaad rakhiye
POINT 1: [One-line takeaway]
POINT 2: [One-line takeaway]
POINT 3: [One-line takeaway]
```

### [SLIDE 9] THE PRACTICAL ACTION
- "Kya karein?" in headline
- 2-3 practical steps a person should take
- Simple, actionable, empowering
- This is the slide people screenshot

**Template:**
```
[SLIDE 9]
HEADING: Kya karein?
STEP 1: [Practical action]
STEP 2: [Practical action]
STEP 3: [Practical action]
```

### [SLIDE 10] THE BRAND CLOSE
- "Sulah" in large serif (48pt)
- "Resolve Your Disputes" in silver (16pt)
- One-line CTA: "Save karke rakho. Share karke help karo."
- No contact info. No solicitation. Pure brand presence.

**Template:**
```
[SLIDE 10]
BRAND: Sulah
TAGLINE: Resolve Your Disputes
CTA: [Educational CTA — save, share, follow]
```

## Narrative Arc — Color Temperature Shift

Across 10 slides, subtly shift the mood:
- Slides 1-2: Slightly warmer midnight (#12202c) — the provocation, tension
- Slides 3-7: Pure midnight (#0f1923) — calm authority, knowledge delivery
- Slides 8-9: Slightly lighter (#162230) — resolution, clarity
- Slide 10: Deepest (#0a1219) — brand gravitas

This shift is subtle — 2-3% brightness variation — but creates an unconscious narrative flow.

| 10 (Brand) | 12 | Brand name |
| **Total** | **~270** | |

## What Sulah Carousels NEVER Do
- No generic stock photos or illustrations
- No clip-art icons
- No gradient backgrounds
- No drop shadows on text
- No more than 2 fonts per carousel
- No bullet points (use line breaks and spacing instead)
- No crowded slides with 4+ ideas
- No solicitation CTAs ever
- No emoji in slide text
- No hashtags on slides (only in caption below)

## What Makes Sulah Carousels Instantly Recognizable
1. The midnight + gold color signature
2. Typography dominance — text IS the visual
3. Generous negative space — the confidence of restraint
4. One-idea-per-slide discipline
5. Legal citations treated as design elements (gold accent, large type)
6. "Sulah" watermark on every slide — quiet brand presence
7. The thin gold separator line — the Sulah signature divider
